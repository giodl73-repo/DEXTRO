COVENANT holds the signatories to their word. In redistricting litigation, the question is not just whether the plan is legally valid but whether its provenance can be proven beyond reasonable challenge. A plan generated by an unverifiable binary, using a derived adjacency file whose chain of custody is opaque, is a plan that opposing counsel can attack on procedural grounds before the court ever reaches the merits. COVENANT prevents this by requiring that every step from Census data to final map is documented, hashable, and independently reproducible.
