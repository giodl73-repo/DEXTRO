## Response to Revision

Round 1 raised three P1 items: (1) the overstated Pareto-optimality claim in the abstract and conclusion, (2) the undifferentiated proportionality measure, and (3) the absent normative engagement with proportionality as a criterion. I also suggested contextualising the 38% county preservation baseline.

**P1.1 — Pareto-optimality overclaim (addressed, well done).** The abstract revision is precise and intellectually honest. The new language — "achieves the best available balance of compactness, VRA compliance, constitutional availability, and geographic stability among the six systems evaluated. Multi-member districts weakly dominate the DIA on proportionality and minority representation but require federal legislation and sacrifice compactness" — is exactly the right framing. It states the empirical finding (non-dominance within the examined portfolio) without asserting global Pareto optimality. The Section 6.2 revision similarly replaces "Pareto-optimal feasible choice" with "best available single-member option" and explicitly states: "This is not a claim of Pareto optimality for all possible systems, but of non-dominance within the examined portfolio." This is a material improvement. Satisfied.

**P1.2 — Proportionality measure directionality (not addressed in this revision).** The proportionality dimension still uses absolute deviation rather than signed deviation, and the distinction between random deviation and systematic Republican seat bonus is not drawn. This remains a real measurement issue. However, it does not affect the Pareto table's main finding (multi-member districts score 3 on proportionality regardless of whether deviation is signed or unsigned), so it is a precision gap rather than an error. I do not lower my score but flag it for the next revision.

**P1.3 — Normative status of proportionality (partially addressed).** The revised Section 3.2 paragraph on scoring rubric mentions that proportionality's "legal status in federal courts is unsettled post-Rucho but available as a state constitutional standard in some states." This is the correct framing. I would have preferred this in the main Section 3 body rather than embedded in the methodology note, but the substance is there. Partially satisfied.

## Residual Concerns

The proportionality directionality issue (signed vs. unsigned deviation) should be addressed in the next round. The current measure obscures a key empirical finding — that the seat bonus is directionally skewed toward Republicans — that is central to the Rodden mechanism discussion.

## Score: 4 — Accept with Minor Revision

The Pareto overclaim revision is the most important fix in this paper, and it is executed cleanly. The remaining issues are genuine but do not prevent acceptance. The paper now accurately represents what the analysis shows and does not overclaim.
