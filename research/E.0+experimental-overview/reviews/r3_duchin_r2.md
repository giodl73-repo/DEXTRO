## Response to Revision

Round 1 raised three P1 items: (1) the underspecified VRA benchmark for minority representation, (2) the unadjusted county preservation metric, and (3) the "resistant to partisan manipulation" criterion not appearing in the Pareto table. I also flagged the Wolpert–Macready citation.

**P1.1 — VRA benchmark specification (not addressed in this revision).** Section 3.1's description of the minority representation dimension still reads "majority-minority districts (MMDs) relative to the VRA Section 2 benchmark derived from Thornburg v. Gingles" without specifying how the benchmark is calculated, what counts as a majority-minority district, or how the Gingles preconditions are operationalised. This is a significant omission. The benchmark calculation is not a minor methodological detail — it is the denominator against which all six systems are scored on the minority representation dimension. Without it, the minority representation column of Table 1 is not independently verifiable. Not satisfied.

**P1.2 — County preservation effective rate (not addressed).** The metric still reads "percentage of counties kept whole" without distinguishing preservable counties from non-preservable ones. The 38% figure conflates counties that are too small or too large relative to district population quotas (which cannot be preserved whole under any method) with counties that could be preserved whole but are not (which is the relevant comparison). Not satisfied.

**P1.3 — Manipulation resistance as criterion (not addressed).** The conclusion still lists "resistant to partisan manipulation" as a defining criterion for the DIA's position without a corresponding Pareto table column or measured outcome. The criterion remains unmeasured and therefore unverifiable. Not satisfied.

**P2 — Wolpert citation (addressed).** The NFL citation has been replaced with a Pareto dominance statement. Correct. Satisfied.

The revisions made in this round address the Pareto overclaim and exchange rate CI — both of which were raised by other reviewers — and add a footnote on Moore v. Harper. These are genuine improvements. However, none of my three P1 items have been addressed. The minority representation measurement specification, the county preservation metric adjustment, and the manipulation-resistance criterion are all unresolved.

But the measurement gaps in Table 1's minority representation and county preservation columns, and the unmeasured manipulation-resistance criterion in the conclusion's Pareto argument, are substantive issues that require revision before the paper can be accepted.
