\begin{abstract}
\noindent
Standard ReCom~\citep{deford2021} samples a random spanning tree of a merged
two-district region and selects a balanced cut, but the acceptance ratio is
always~1: the forward and reverse proposal probabilities are implicitly equal.
This means the stationary distribution is unknown.
Merge-Split MCMC~\citep{janson2022} breaks this symmetry by sampling an
\emph{independent} reverse spanning tree and computing the ratio
$\mathrm{valid\_cuts\_fwd} / \mathrm{valid\_cuts\_rev}$, which equals
$Q(\pi' \!\to\! \pi) / Q(\pi \!\to\! \pi')$ exactly under uniform cut
selection.
The resulting chain approximately targets the uniform distribution over valid
redistricting plans at $O(m \log m)$ per step---the same asymptotic cost as
standard ReCom and far cheaper than Forest ReCom's $O(m^2)$ determinant
computation~\citep{dellaLibera2026forestRecom}.
We implement \mergesplit{} as \texttt{MergeSplitChain} and expose it via
\texttt{--search merge-split}.
Empirical results on North Carolina ($k=14$) and Wisconsin ($k=8$) show an
acceptance rate of approximately~60\%, higher than Forest ReCom's~40\%,
with comparable ensemble quality.
\mergesplit{} is the recommended chain when approximate uniformity is
sufficient and speed matters; Forest ReCom is reserved for applications
requiring stricter distributional guarantees.
\end{abstract}
