\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

Merge-Split MCMC~\citep{janson2022} resolves the stationary distribution
ambiguity of standard \recom{}~\citep{deford2021} by sampling an independent
reverse spanning tree and computing the ratio
$\mathrm{cuts\_fwd} / \mathrm{cuts\_rev}$ as the Metropolis-Hastings
acceptance probability.
The forward cut count appears in the numerator because it dilutes the
forward proposal probability; the resulting ratio equals
$Q(\pi' \!\to\! \pi) / Q(\pi \!\to\! \pi')$ in expectation, giving
approximate detailed balance with the uniform distribution as the target.

The implementation as \texttt{MergeSplitChain} achieves $O(m \log m)$
per-step cost---the same asymptotic cost as standard \recom{} and far below
\forestrecom{}'s $O(m^2)$---while raising distributional precision from
``unknown stationary distribution'' to ``approximately uniform.''
The empirical acceptance rate of $\approx\!60\%$ on NC and WI is intermediate
between standard \recom{} ($\approx\!80\%$) and \forestrecom{} ($\approx\!40\%$),
reflecting the two-tree heuristic's intermediate strictness.

\mergesplit{} is the recommended chain for production redistricting
ensembles where approximate uniformity is sufficient and ensemble size
or wall-clock budget makes \forestrecom{} impractical.
\forestrecom{} is reserved for applications requiring exact theoretical
guarantees; SMC~\citep{dellaLibera2026smc} is reserved for exact
calibration.
The implementation is available via \texttt{--search merge-split}.
