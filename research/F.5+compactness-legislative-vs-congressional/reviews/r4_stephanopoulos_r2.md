## Response to Revision

The abstract-body consistency correction is the most important change in this revision. The remaining concerns I raised in R1 are partially addressed.

**C1 (Polsby-Popper as sole compactness measure)** — Not addressed. The paper still uses only Polsby-Popper. For maximum legal utility, a Reock score comparison (Section 3.4) would demonstrate that the compactness advantage is robust to metric choice. Courts in different states have used different measures, and a paper intending to be used across multiple state litigations should show consistency across metrics. This remains the most important unaddressed concern.

**C2 (Gerrymandering classification sourcing)** — Not addressed. Section 5 still identifies "states where the enacted map is identified by courts or analysts as gerrymandered" without a sourced classification table. Without specific court decisions or academic analyses cited for each state, this claim remains insufficiently documented for litigation use.

**C4 (VRA-mode caveat for comparison maps)** — Addressed. A footnote has been added noting that VRA mode is disabled for all F.5 maps to isolate compactness effects, and that for states with Section 2 requirements, the operative algorithmic map would have VRA mode enabled, reducing compactness in minority districts. This is the correct disclosure.

**C3 (Cross-census comparison to 2020 enacted maps)** — Not addressed. The cross-cycle comparison issue (2000 and 2010 algorithmic maps compared to 2020 enacted maps) is not discussed. This is a methodological limitation that should be disclosed.

## Assessment

The internal consistency fix is significant. I maintain 3/4 because the two most legally important gaps (Reock score robustness check, gerrymandering classification sourcing) remain open.

**Score**: 3/4
**Recommendation**: Accept with minor revisions
