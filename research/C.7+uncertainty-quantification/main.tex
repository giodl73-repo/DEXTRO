Confidence Intervals for Compactness, Partisan Fairness,\\

\large C.7 --- Validation Track: Uncertainty Quantification}

\author{Gio Della-Libera \\ \textit{Apportionment Research Group}}

\date{May 2026}

\begin{abstract}
Point estimates of redistricting performance metrics---Polsby-Popper
compactness, efficiency gap, minority VAP percentages---are insufficient
for legal and policy use without accompanying confidence intervals (CIs).
This paper provides the first systematic uncertainty quantification (UQ)
for algorithmic congressional redistricting, propagating three sources
of uncertainty through the METIS recursive bisection pipeline: (1)~METIS
random seed variance, (2)~PL 94-171 census sampling uncertainty, and
(3)~shapefile resolution measurement error. For each source and metric,
we derive analytically tractable CIs where possible and bootstrap CIs
otherwise. Key results: METIS seed variance contributes CV $<$ 2\% to
Polsby-Popper for 96\% of states (48/50), yielding bootstrap
bootstrap CIs of $[0.351, 0.370]$ at 95\% for the national mean PP
(point estimate 0.361). Census undercount uncertainty (0.3--1.1\%
by state) propagates to $\pm 0.002$ on district population deviation
via the delta method. Resolution-induced measurement error for PP is
$\Delta PP < 0.005$ mean across a 130$\times$ unit-count range. The
$+22\%$ compactness improvement over enacted plans is robust: accounting
for all three uncertainty sources yields a 95\% CI of $[+15\%, +29\%]$.
Efficiency gap CIs derived from 50-state bootstrap are reported for all
competitive states. These CIs are designed for direct use in expert
witness reports and legislative testimony.
\end{abstract}

\noindent\textbf{Keywords:} uncertainty quantification, confidence intervals,
redistricting, bootstrap, delta method, efficiency gap, Polsby-Popper,
VRA, census undercount
