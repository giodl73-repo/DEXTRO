# Review 2 — Jonathan Rodden (Partisan Implications / Electoral Geography Lens)
**Paper**: D.4 — Adopting Algorithmic Congressional Redistricting: Legal Pathways, Constitutional Constraints, and Model Legislation
**Round**: R2
**Score**: 4/4 (Accept)

## Summary

The revision addresses the three Priority 1 statute deficiencies that were the structural basis for my Round 1 concerns. The VRA mode provision (§2(e)) is added. The compactness note resolves the METIS/Polsby-Popper mismatch. The Gundy and Miller paragraphs in Section 6 address the post-2019 constitutional risk landscape. My Priority 3 concerns — the partisan tilt footnote, the 69-district disaggregation, and the political feasibility discussion — remain substantially unaddressed, but these were advisory rather than structural and do not prevent acceptance.

## Response to Round 1 Issues

**P1-A (VRA mode provision — addressed).** The new §2(e) VRA Adjustment is appropriate. From an electoral geography perspective, the critical provision is §2(e)(3): ``partisan affiliation data is not used in determining the adjustment, consistent with *Louisiana v. Callais*, 608 U.S. ___ (2026).'' This directly addresses the *Callais* problem — the Callais court's difficulty was that it could not disentangle whether the district at issue was driven by VRA Section 2 compliance or by partisan advantage. The statutory prohibition on partisan data in VRA adjustments, combined with the Gingles justification requirement in §2(e)(2), creates the documentary record that the paper's *Callais* argument requires. Resolved.

**P1-B (Compactness metric mismatch — addressed).** The Drafting Notes paragraph on compactness measurement is accurate and legally useful. The key legal contribution is the proposed definition: ``maximum compactness'' as minimum-edge-cut partition of the TIGER adjacency graph. This is an objectively verifiable criterion: courts challenging on compactness grounds would evaluate whether the algorithm was run correctly on the published adjacency graph. This is far more administrable than a Polsby-Popper threshold that depends on shapefile details. Resolved.

**Gundy and Miller additions (addressed via P2-B and P2-C).** The Section 6 additions are well-integrated. The Gundy paragraph correctly identifies the specific justices whose opinions signal potential doctrine change and explains why the algorithmic redistricting mandate differs categorically from the type of delegation that concerns them: the Bureau exercises no policy discretion — it runs a deterministic computation. The Miller v. Johnson paragraph correctly applies the predominant-factor test and explains how the statute's design (base partition produced without demographic data + separately documented VRA adjustment) creates the analytical separability that *Bethune-Hill* requires. These are genuine improvements.

## Residual Concerns (Not Blocking)

**P3-A (Partisan tilt footnote).** The observation that geographic sorting produces a systematic Democratic advantage from compactness optimization remains in footnote 2. This is the central political objection to the paper's proposal and it deserves engagement in the main text. The paper's legal audience — legislators, judges, and their clerks — will encounter this objection from political actors who oppose reform. The paper would be stronger for addressing it head-on, with the observation that this tilt exists in commission-drawn and court-ordered maps as well and reflects geography, not algorithmic design.

**P3-B (69-district disaggregation).** The aggregate 69-district figure still does not distinguish states where the algorithmic increase in majority-minority districts corrects Republican-gerrymander fragmentation from states where the decrease corrects Democratic-gerrymander over-packing. A table showing this breakdown by state partisan control at time of enacted plan would substantially strengthen the VRA argument.

## Recommendation

Accept. The three P1 structural deficiencies are resolved. The constitutional analysis is the paper's core contribution and it is now stronger for the Gundy and Miller additions.
