## Response to Revision

Round 1 raised three P1 items: (1) the 0.015 vs. 0.019 exchange rate inconsistency, (2) the concavity claim from three data points, and (3) the undocumented 20% irreducibility bound.

**P1.1 — Exchange rate inconsistency (addressed, well done).** The revision is precise and satisfying. Section 6.1 now labels the two figures explicitly: "within-system exchange rate (E.5-derived): 0.019 PP/pp" and "cross-system exchange rate (E.0 synthesis): 0.015 PP/pp," with an explanation that these measure different phenomena. The justification for using 0.015 as the headline throughout is well-stated: it characterises the frontier across the full portfolio, not only the within-algorithm parametric trajectory. The first paragraph of Lesson 5 also restates 0.015 as the median state-level rate (range 0.011–0.023), which adds geographic context that makes the number more interpretable. This revision resolves the inconsistency and adds explanatory depth. Satisfied.

**P1.2 — Concavity from three data points (addressed, satisfactory).** Section 3.4 now reads: "The data suggest a concave relationship across the three tested configurations (single-member, 3-member, 5-member districts), consistent with diminishing returns... With only three data points, the functional form cannot be confirmed, but the pattern is sufficient to support the policy conclusion that the Fair Representation Act's 3--5 member specification captures most of the available improvement." This is the correct statistical qualification — it changes "is concave" (a factual claim) to "suggest a concave relationship... consistent with" (an evidential claim). The acknowledgment that the functional form cannot be confirmed is honest and correct. Satisfied.

**P1.3 — 20% irreducibility bound (not addressed).** Section 3.3 still reads "derived from the fraction of Democratic votes that are in census tracts with >70% Democratic composition" without providing the calculation, the national fraction, the translation to the 20% bound, or a specific E.1 citation. This is an unvalidated statistic. Not satisfied.

## Assessment of Statistical Improvements

The exchange rate clarification resolves the paper's most significant internal consistency problem. The within-system / cross-system distinction is the correct conceptual resolution and maps clearly onto the two data sources. The concavity qualification transforms an overclaimed finding into an honest evidential summary. Both are model examples of how to report preliminary empirical patterns responsibly.

The 20% irreducibility bound remains undocumented. This is a specific number used to ground a major claim (the theoretical maximum sorting gap reduction from multi-member districting) and it needs a calculation, not just a prose description of the methodology.
