## Response to Revision

The "4 of 5" → "all 5" correction is the most important change in this revision and it has been carried through consistently: abstract, introduction, Table 1 bottom row, the discussion paragraph, and the conclusion. The paper now correctly states that the 42% threshold achieves majority-minority districts in all 5 covered states at state house scale (compared to 4 of 5 at congressional scale, where South Carolina fails). This is a stronger finding than the original paper claimed.

**C1 (Political relevance of state legislative VRA)** — Addressed. The introduction now includes a paragraph connecting the Callais framework to state legislative redistricting and noting that algorithmic state legislative maps offer the same Callais disentanglement advantage as algorithmic congressional maps. The connection to post-Shaw race-conscious mapping pressures is now stated.

**C2 (Wisconsin not in analysis)** — Addressed with a footnote noting that Wisconsin is not included because its precinct-level data from 2020 is not available in the Kuriwaki interpolated dataset at block-group resolution, and noting that Wisconsin's Milwaukee County has substantial Black population that may trigger state house Section 2 obligations.

**C3 (Georgia minority group composition)** — Addressed. A footnote now clarifies that Georgia's 55 majority-minority districts include both majority-Black and majority-Hispanic districts, with geographic concentration notes. The analysis treats the two groups separately under independent Gingles frameworks.

**C4 (Partisan implications of MM district creation)** — Not addressed. The paper still does not discuss the "bleaching" or "packing" effect of MM district creation on neighboring districts' partisan composition. For states creating many MM districts (55 in Georgia, 48 in Texas), this effect could be quantitatively significant. A brief note would be appropriate.

## Assessment

The three substantive corrections (5/5 finding, Callais-state connection, Georgia minority group clarification) are exactly what this paper needed. I maintain 3/4 because the partisan implications of MM district creation (C4) remain unaddressed and is relevant to understanding the full political implications of VRA-compliant algorithmic redistricting.

**Score**: 3/4
**Recommendation**: Accept with minor revisions
