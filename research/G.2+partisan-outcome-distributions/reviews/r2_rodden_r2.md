# Review: G.2 — Partisan Outcome Distributions
**Reviewer**: Jonathan Rodden (Political geography, partisan sorting)
**Round**: 2
**Score**: 3/4

## Summary

The revision adds the critical acknowledgment that ensemble-median outcomes in sorted states are not proportional, and that the AR plan's near-median position establishes geographic consistency rather than partisan neutrality. This is exactly the framing I requested and it is correctly placed in both the abstract and the body. The overdispersion direction correction is also right. The paper is now the most accurate treatment of the relationship between compact redistricting and partisan outcomes I have seen in this literature.

## Changes Evaluated

**Rodden effect framing (B2):** The revised abstract and the added paragraph after the results table correctly and prominently distinguish "geographic consistency" from "proportional fairness." The specific statement — "In geographically sorted states, the ensemble median itself reflects Republican over-representation relative to vote share" — is accurate and important. This framing is necessary for the paper to avoid being misused as a claim that the AR plan produces fair partisan outcomes in all states.

**Underdispersion correction (B1):** Correct. The contiguity + population balance explanation for why sorted states are underdispersed relative to the binomial is the right mechanism. The numerical example is clear. This is a contribution to the ensemble methodology literature beyond the redistricting context.

**Georgia corridor analysis:** The revised Section 3 correctly addresses the question of whether compact plans with 6D are achievable: 28% of DeFord ensemble plans achieve 6D+, and the AR algorithm trades compactness for the one Democratic seat that would have been achievable with less compact boundaries. This is honest and correct.

## Residual Concern

The paper still does not adequately address what I raised in my earlier minor issue: the Georgia $p_{\rm geo} \approx 0.36$ corresponds to an expected seat count under the pure binomial of approximately $14 \times 0.36 = 5.04$ — essentially 5D. But the paper states the ensemble median is 6D. These are consistent only if the underdispersion correction shifts the mean upward, or if $p_{\rm geo}$ is calculated differently from what the paper states. The relationship between $p_{\rm geo}$ and the ensemble mean should be made explicit. This is a moderate clarification, not a blocking issue.

**Minnesota (H1):** The \est{} marker is present but the corridor fraction of 59% is not consistent with the pure binomial prediction of ~71% for a competitive 8-seat state. The methodology needs a clearer statement.

## Recommendation

Accept with minor revisions. The paper is now technically correct on the blocking and most high-priority issues. Clarify the $p_{\rm geo}$ vs. ensemble-mean relationship for Georgia and the Minnesota estimation methodology.
