## Review: PercentileSweep — Statutory Choice of Legal Posture in Algorithmic Redistricting (Round 2)

### Response to Round 1 Concerns

**D-P1-A (statute box bisection/ensemble conflation — blocking)**: Resolved. The revised Posture 2 statute box in §5.2 now contains the required qualification sentence:

> "Note: this representativeness claim holds within the bisection family (plans produced by the same SHA-256 seed walk). It is not a claim that the plan is typical among all valid redistricting plans---that stronger claim requires ensemble comparison (see G.1)."

This is the exact fix I requested and it is correctly placed inside the statute box where a court or legislator reading only that box will encounter it. The legal malpractice risk I identified in Round 1 is removed. This is the most important change in the revision and it has been executed correctly. I raise my score on this basis.

**D-P1-B (§5.1 paragraph order — insensitivity before zero-sum argument)**: Not resolved. The current §5.1 still leads with "Compactness is not a zero-sum quantity: maximising compactness does not require minimising anything else" before reaching the insensitivity argument. The revision did not restructure this paragraph order. The circular argument still precedes the substantive response. This is a moderate concern — the content is all present — but the rhetorical order still presents a weaker argument first and buries the stronger one. This remains an unresolved P1 from Round 1.

**D-P1-C (G.1 ensemble percentiles as point estimates)**: Not resolved. §5.1 still states "the challenger must produce a plan more compact than 99.8% of all valid plans" as an adversarial bar without noting that this bar is contingent on G.1's sampling adequacy. The qualification that G.1 ensemble percentiles are point estimates without confidence bounds was requested in Round 1 and has not been added.

**D-P1-D (VRA and non-compactness redistricting criteria)**: Not resolved. Neither §5 nor any new subsection acknowledges that legal posture analysis is restricted to compactness, population balance, and contiguity, or that additional state-level criteria (county lines, communities of interest, VRA) may interact with percentile selection. The §6.3 future-work section mentions "multi-constraint variants (VrASection, AreaSection)" but does not engage the legal posture implications. The Callais cross-reference I requested is absent from §5.

### Assessment of Round 2 State

The blocking concern (D-P1-A) is resolved. The paper can now be submitted without the legal malpractice risk I identified in Round 1. However, three of my four P1 items remain open. D-P1-B (paragraph order) is a structural issue that weakens the §5.1 argument without breaking it. D-P1-C (G.1 qualification) is a genuine intellectual integrity concern — the adversarial bar argument should be qualified. D-P1-D (VRA interaction) is a substantive gap for any state with significant minority populations.

The revision addressed the single most critical item cleanly and correctly. The remaining three items are real but, in my judgment, no longer individually blocking given that D-P1-A has been resolved.

### Score

**3 / 4** — Accept with revisions. The bisection/ensemble statute box conflation (D-P1-A) is fixed — this was my blocking concern and it is now correctly handled. The remaining three open items (paragraph order, G.1 qualification, VRA interaction) reduce the paper's quality but do not make it a malpractice risk. I recommend acceptance conditional on D-P1-B (paragraph reorder) and D-P1-D (VRA acknowledgment paragraph) being addressed before final publication.
