## Methodological Rigor

### Statistical Methods Used
1. **Parametric tests**: t-tests (paired and two-sample)
2. **Bootstrap**: 10,000 resamples for non-parametric CIs
3. **Permutation**: 10,000 permutations for distribution-free testing
4. **Effect sizes**: Cohen's d for magnitude assessment

### Why Multiple Methods?
- **t-tests**: Standard, powerful under normality
- **Bootstrap**: Robust to departures from normality, no distributional assumptions
- **Permutation**: Most robust, tests exact null hypothesis without parametric assumptions
- **Convergence across methods** → Confidence in findings

### Interpretation Standards
- **p-value thresholds**: α = 0.05 (standard), α = 0.01 (conservative)
- **Effect size thresholds**: |d| < 0.2 (negligible), 0.2-0.5 (small), 0.5-0.8 (medium), ≥0.8 (large)
- **CI interpretation**: Excludes zero → significant effect
- **Multiple comparisons**: Bonferroni correction considered (α_corrected = 0.01)

### Limitations Acknowledged
1. **Small state sample**: n = 5 limits cross-state power
2. **Between-state heterogeneity**: Obscures aggregate patterns
3. **Small effect sizes**: Cohen's d = 0.169 (small in absolute terms)
4. **Multiple tests**: 5 primary tests, Bonferroni may be overly conservative
