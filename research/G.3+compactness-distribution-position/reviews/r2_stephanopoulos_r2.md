## Outstanding Legal Issues

**Miller v. Johnson and Bush v. Vera engagement (M1 from REVISION-PLAN): Not fully added.** I asked for a paragraph on the Shaw line cases noting that irregular shapes are "probative but not dispositive" of racial sorting. The Section 6 additions address the manageability objection but do not discuss the Shaw/Miller/Vera doctrine on compactness as a racial gerrymandering indicator. This matters because the AR plan's above-median compactness could be characterized as indicating racial sorting in states with concentrated minority populations (NC, GA). The paper should note that the 61st–72nd percentile range falls well within the range of non-suspicious plans under Shaw doctrine.

**Edge-cut vs. Polsby-Popper as legal metric (my Round 1 concern):** Still underdeveloped. The paper should note that courts use PP (not edge cut) as the compactness metric, and that the AR plan's PP score is what would be examined in litigation.

## Recommendation

Accept with minor revisions. The blocking issues are resolved and the paper is substantially improved. Add the Shaw/Miller discussion on racial gerrymandering implications of compactness, and clarify the edge-cut vs. PP distinction for legal audiences.
