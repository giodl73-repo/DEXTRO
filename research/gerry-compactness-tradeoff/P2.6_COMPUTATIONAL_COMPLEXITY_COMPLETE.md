## Key Technical Contributions

### 1. Theoretical Complexity Validation

**METIS algorithm complexity**:
- Coarsening: O(|E|)
- Initial partitioning: O(|V|log|V|)
- Refinement: O(|E|log|V|)
- **Total**: O(|E| + |V|log|V|) per bisection

**For k districts**:
- Recursive bisection depth: log₂(k) levels
- **Total complexity**: O(k(|E| + |V|log|V|))
- **Interpretation**: Near-linear in practice (k small, log|V| slow-growing)

### 2. Empirical Runtime Measurements

**State-level results**:
| State | Tracts | Baseline (s) | Edge-Weighted (s) | Overhead |
|-------|--------|-------------|-------------------|----------|
| MS    | 880    | 0.15        | 0.17              | 1.13×    |
| LA    | 1,140  | 0.32        | 0.36              | 1.13×    |
| AL    | 1,180  | 0.35        | 0.39              | 1.11×    |
| SC    | 1,100  | 0.31        | 0.35              | 1.13×    |
| GA    | 1,960  | 0.98        | 1.10              | 1.12×    |
| **Mean** | | **0.42** | **0.47** | **1.12×** |

**Key Finding**: Edge-weighting adds **only 12% overhead**, confirming negligible computational cost.

### 3. Scalability Analysis

**Tract-level → Block-level extrapolation**:
- Tract-level: 1,400 nodes average, 0.42s mean runtime
- Block-level: 50,000 nodes (35× increase)
- Scaling factor with O(n log n): 35 × (log 50,000 / log 1,400) ≈ 50×
- **Extrapolated block-level runtime**: 0.42s × 50 ≈ 21 seconds

**Implication**: Block-level optimization (finer geographic precision) remains computationally feasible at <25s per state.

### 4. Practical Applicability Assessment

**Parameter sweep cost**:
- 105 configurations tested (21 per state × 5 states)
- Total runtime: 105 × 0.47s ≈ 50 seconds (<1 minute)
- With post-processing: <1 hour total experimental time

**National scalability**:
- 50 states × 0.47s ≈ 24 seconds (tract-level, single configuration)
- Full parameter sweep (21 configs): ~8 minutes
- **Parallelizable**: With 4-8 cores, <2 minutes total

**Interactive tools**:
- Sub-second runtimes enable real-time parameter adjustment
- Users can explore tradeoffs dynamically (adjust β, τ, see immediate results)
- Supports stakeholder engagement during redistricting process

### 5. Comparison to Ensemble Methods

**MCMC/ReCom computational cost**:
- Generate 10,000 plans per state
- Alabama ensemble: ~2 hours (ReCom, 10K plans)
- Required for statistical validation, not practical optimization

**Edge-weighted METIS**:
- Single optimized plan: 0.47s
- **Speedup**: ~15,000× faster than ensemble generation
- Near-Pareto-optimal (0.4% dominated by ensemble)

**Implication**: Edge-weighted METIS offers "good enough" optimization (near-optimal) at 1000× lower computational cost. Ensemble methods valuable for validation, not production redistricting.
