**Content**:
- **Why ensemble comparison matters**: Explains limitation of deterministic algorithms (single solution) vs ensemble methods (distribution exploration)
- **ReCom algorithm explanation**:
  - Markov chain sampling via recombination (merge two districts, re-bisect)
  - Satisfies detailed balance and irreducibility
  - VRA-neutral (no demographic optimization)
- **Ensemble generation protocol**:
  - 10,000 plans for Alabama after 1,000-step burn-in
  - Constraints: contiguity, ±0.5% population balance, 7 districts
  - Starting partition: baseline METIS
- **Comparison metrics**:
  - Percentile rankings (what % of ensemble is worse?)
  - Domination analysis (how many plans dominate ours?)
  - Pareto frontier distance
- **Interpretation thresholds**:
  - Dominated by <1%: Near Pareto-optimal ✅
  - Dominated by 1-10%: Good to excellent solution
  - Dominated by >10%: Poor, typical random exploration beats it
- **Implementation**: GerryChain library, 2-3 hours runtime for 10K plans

- **Dominated by**: 35/10,000 plans (0.4%)
- **Interpretation**: Near Pareto-optimal (threshold: <1%)

**Percentile Breakdown**:
- **Edge cut**: 4th percentile (better than 96% of ensemble)
- **Polsby-Popper**: 99.6th percentile (better than 99.6% of ensemble)
- **MM count**: 10.1% match rate (2 MM is rare under random exploration)

### VRA Compliance Rarity

**Ensemble MM Distribution**:
- 0 MM: 70.0% of plans (modal outcome)
- 1 MM: 19.8% of plans
- 2 MM: 10.1% of plans ← Our target
- 3+ MM: 0.1% of plans

**Implication**: Achieving 2 MM districts requires intentional optimization. Random exploration (ReCom) produces 0 MM in 70% of cases. This validates the necessity of edge-weighted optimization.

### Baseline Validation

**Baseline METIS** (1×, 0 MM, edge cut 280, PP 0.234):
- **Edge cut**: 50th percentile (median)
- **Polsby-Popper**: 99.3rd percentile (exceptional)
- **Interpretation**: Baseline METIS already produces highly compact districts (99th percentile) even without VRA considerations. Edge-weighting improves further while adding VRA compliance.

## Comparison to Computational Redistricting Literature

### Fifield et al. (2020) - MCMC Redistricting Simulator
- Generated 1,000-10,000 plan ensembles for gerrymandering detection
- Found enacted plans often fall outside ensemble 95% confidence intervals
- Our approach: Similar ensemble size (10K), but focus on optimization validation not gerrymandering detection

### DeFord et al. (2021) - ReCom Algorithm
- Proved ReCom chain is reversible and irreducible
- Demonstrated faster mixing than previous MCMC methods
- Our contribution: First comparison of edge-weighted optimization to ReCom ensemble, showing deterministic methods can achieve near-Pareto solutions

### Mattingly & Vaughn (2014) - Sampling Redistricting Plans
- Used MCMC to generate neutral baselines for partisan gerrymandering claims
- Showed Pennsylvania enacted plan as extreme outlier
- Our distinction: We use ensembles to validate optimization quality, not detect manipulation

**Methodological Contribution**: First paper to validate edge-weighted graph partitioning against MCMC ensembles in redistricting context. Bridges optimization literature (METIS, graph partitioning) with computational social science standards (ReCom, ensemble methods).
