# REVISION PLAN — D.5: Gingles Bloc-Voting Methodology
**Round 1 Panel Scores:** Karypis 3/4 | Rodden 3/4 | Duchin 3/4 | Stephanopoulos 2/4 | Liang 3/4
**Average:** 2.8/4
**Status:** Major revisions required — Stephanopoulos's 2/4 and three identified legal errors block publication

## Critical Issues (Must Fix Before R2)

### C1 — Callais Mischaracterization [Stephanopoulos, primary]
The paper overstates what *Callais* required by saying it "added a specific methodological requirement" that expert witnesses "must" use WLS+HC3+Holm with a partisan control. *Callais* established a substantive requirement (disentangle racial from partisan bloc voting); this paper proposes a specific statistical implementation of that requirement. The distinction matters: an opposing expert using a different methodology (King EI with partisan controls, or Bayesian ecological inference with covariates) could also satisfy the *Callais* standard, and the paper should not imply otherwise.
**Action:** Revise all language that says *Callais* "requires" WLS+HC3+Holm to say that *Callais* requires disentanglement of racial from partisan bloc voting, and that this paper presents one specific implementation using WLS+HC3+Holm. Add a sentence acknowledging that other methodologies satisfying the disentanglement requirement are also permissible.

### C2 — Prong 1 Threshold Legal Calibration [Stephanopoulos, primary; Karypis secondary]
The 0.5 alignment score threshold for Prong 1 is a critical legal claim attributed to "dellaLibera2026threshold," an unpublished and unavailable paper. The claim that "all majority-minority districts generated by VRASection for the 2020 census have alignment scores above 0.55, and that districts in states where Prong 1 has been litigated and accepted by courts have alignment scores averaging 0.71" is the entire empirical basis for the threshold.
**Action:** Include the threshold calibration analysis directly in this paper. Report the distribution of alignment scores for (a) all VRASection-generated majority-minority districts nationally (2020 census), (b) districts from states where Prong 1 has been litigated and courts have accepted the district (cite the specific cases), and (c) districts where Prong 1 has been litigated and courts rejected the district. The 0.5 threshold should be derived from the observed distribution, not cited to an unavailable paper.

### C3 — Allen v. Milligan Post-Remand Update [Stephanopoulos, primary]
The paper uses the Alabama AL-07 case from the original *Allen v. Milligan* complaint. But *Allen* produced a remedial order requiring Alabama to draw a second majority-Black district, and the final remedial map was approved in 2024 (after *Allen v. Milligan* post-remand). The worked example should use the remedial map context — the setting where the methodology is actually needed — not the pre-litigation enacted map.
**Action:** Update the Alabama worked example to reflect the *Allen* post-remand context: the analysis should be framed as demonstrating that the remedial algorithm output satisfies all three Gingles prongs, which is the actual use case (special master or expert witness producing a remedial map). Describe the algorithmic output that would have satisfied the *Allen* remedial order.

### C4 — VIF Reporting for Prong 3 [Stephanopoulos, primary; Liang secondary]
The Callais disentanglement assumption (the partisan control variable captures all partisan effects, leaving only racial effects in β_race) is subject to multicollinearity concerns in high-racial-polarization states like Alabama. The correlation between X_maj (majority racial composition) and P_t (Republican presidential vote share) needs to be reported.
**Action:** Report the correlation between X_maj_t and P_t for the Alabama precinct sample, and the variance inflation factor (VIF) for the Prong 3 regression. If VIF > 10, add a multicollinearity robustness discussion: report the condition number of the design matrix and consider presenting results separately for precincts with low vs. high correlation between racial composition and partisan vote.

## Important Issues (Should Fix Before R2)

### I1 — Bootstrap Specification [Liang, primary; Karypis secondary]
Section 6.2 describes bootstrap confidence intervals without specifying: number of replicates, clustering level (precinct vs. county), or interval type (percentile vs. BCa).
**Action:** Add a methods paragraph specifying: (a) 1000 bootstrap replicates (standard for CI estimation), (b) county-clustered bootstrap to account for spatial autocorrelation (sampling counties with replacement, then all precincts within selected counties), (c) percentile CI method. Report the county-clustered vs. unclustered CI widths for one Alabama election to demonstrate the effect of clustering.

### I2 — COI Identification Algorithm [Karypis, primary]
The VRASection alignment score (Equation 2.1) depends on a "community of interest" identified by the VRASection algorithm, but the COI identification method is not described.
**Action:** Add a subsection describing the COI identification algorithm: connected components on high-minority-CVAP tracts (threshold: ≥ 40% minority CVAP), with the largest connected component identified as the COI. Specify the threshold and the connectivity criterion (queen contiguity vs. rook contiguity). Justify the threshold with reference to the 40% threshold used in the VRA boost weight parameter.

### I3 — Alabama LOO Table [Duchin, primary]
The worked example states "the LOO analysis shows that excluding any single election does not change the Prong 3 conclusion" without showing the LOO table.
**Action:** Include the LOO table for Alabama in Section 5.3. For each of the eight elections analyzed, show the Prong 3 coefficient β̂_race estimated excluding that election, and the Holm-corrected p-value for the remaining seven. The LOO stability claim should be verifiable from the table.

### I4 — Ensemble-Based Prong 1 Interpretation [Duchin, primary]
The paper does not clarify whether the ensemble-based Prong 1 robustness check asks: (a) does some district in the ensemble have alignment > 0.5, or (b) does the same geographic district consistently appear with alignment > 0.5.
**Action:** Clarify in Section 6.2 that the ensemble analysis asks: "across ensemble configurations, what fraction have a district whose geographic core matches the minority community and whose alignment score exceeds 0.5?" This is option (a), which answers the Prong 1 question "can a compliant district be drawn?" Frame the question and the answer clearly.

## Moderate Issues (Should Fix Before R2)

### M1 — King EI Model Justification [Rodden, primary]
The paper uses WLS ecological regression rather than King's (1997) EI model. For legal expert testimony, the choice of estimator should be explicitly justified.
**Action:** Add a paragraph justifying the WLS choice: (a) WLS is more computationally tractable for 50-state deployment, (b) HC3 standard errors provide valid inference under WLS even with heteroskedasticity, and (c) King EI assumes a specific distributional model that may not hold for Alabama precinct data. Note that the LOO stability analysis provides the key robustness check that is more directly interpretable than the distributional assumptions of King EI.

### M2 — Holm vs. Benjamini-Hochberg [Duchin]
The paper acknowledges in Section 8 that B-H may be more appropriate than Holm in high-correlation election samples, but presents this only as a limitation.
**Action:** Move the Holm vs. B-H discussion from the limitations to the methodology section. Add the justification for Holm's conservatism being desirable in a legal context: if a finding survives a conservative correction, it is harder to challenge in court. Report B-H corrected p-values alongside Holm p-values for Alabama as a robustness check.

### M3 — Geographic Description of Black Belt District [Rodden]
The paper's geographic description of the new Black Belt district ("from the Georgia border toward Mobile") appears imprecise for the Black Belt geography.
**Action:** Verify the geographic description of the algorithmic Black Belt district against actual county assignments. Black Belt counties (Wilcox, Dallas, Lowndes, Marengo, Greene, Hale, Perry) run east-west across south-central Alabama; a district connecting them toward Mobile would be in southwest Alabama, not from the Georgia border. Correct the description or provide a map.
