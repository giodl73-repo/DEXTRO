\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We have introduced subdivision-respecting redistricting: a single stickiness
parameter $\alpha$ that formally operationalises the statutory requirement to
preserve political subdivisions in algorithmic redistricting.
The parameter is derived from TIGER GEOID data alone, requires no partisan
inputs, composes with any split strategy and any other edge-weight signal,
and is independently verifiable from public data.

At $\alpha = 5$, county splits fall by 45\% on average across all 50 states,
the edge-cut increases by a factor of 2.5, and there is no systematic partisan
effect.
The algorithm respects existing county lines simply because those lines already
exist and are cheaper to cut along than through counties.

The stickiness framework generalises beyond counties to any level of the
governmental subdivision hierarchy (MCDs, incorporated places, voting precincts),
providing a unified mechanism for statutory compliance across states with
different primary subdivision requirements.
This makes it directly applicable to state legislative redistricting, which
often has stricter subdivision-preservation requirements than congressional
redistricting.

Future work will study the full $\alpha$ sweep across multiple seeds (convergence),
extend to MCD and place-level stickiness using the COUSUB and PLACE TIGER files,
and examine the interaction between subdivision stickiness and VRA compliance.
