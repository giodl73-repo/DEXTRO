# Review R-1: Moon Duchin
**Paper**: The Proportionality Compromise
**Date**: 2026-05-03
**Score**: 3.0 / 4

The Lorenz-feasibility framing is the paper's best idea. Reframing the proportionality gap as a geometric packing problem rather than a calibration failure is technically correct and useful in the litigation context. The σ classification (free/cheap/expensive) is compact and communicable.

**Concerns:** The paper claims the partisan Lorenz curve "determines geometric feasibility" without a formal sufficiency proof. The σ<0.05 finding for GA/WI is surprising given known geographic sorting; needs to be reconciled against *Why Cities Lose* directly. The C in C×σ bound is not derived — is it universal or state-dependent?

**P1:**
1. Prove or tighten the C×σ bound — show whether C is universal or state-dependent.
2. Add formal proof sketch for Lorenz feasibility claim, or clearly label as conjecture.
3. Cross-validate GA/WI gaps against enacted-plan B.9 data.
