\begin{abstract}
  % TODO: write abstract after sections are drafted
  The Districting Integrity Act proposes that each state draw its congressional
  districts by executing a deterministic graph-bisection algorithm with a
  single federally published random seed. This paper asks: how much does seed
  choice matter? Because graph minimum bisection is NP-hard, the METIS
  multilevel heuristic finds a local optimum whose identity depends on the
  seed used in the initial partitioning phase. We characterize the
  \emph{near-optimal solution space} --- the set of plans within $\varepsilon$
  of the minimum edge cut --- and measure the distribution of partisan outcomes
  (proportionality gap, efficiency gap, seat allocation) across 1{,}000 seeds
  per state for all 50 states. [Results TBD].
\end{abstract}
