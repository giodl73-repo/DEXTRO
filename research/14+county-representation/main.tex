\begin{abstract}
Redistricting creates artificial boundaries prone to gerrymandering and litigation. This paper explores an alternative: allocating congressional representatives directly to existing counties based on population, using weighted voting to achieve proportional representation. We analyze 3,143 U.S. counties to compare county-based representation against algorithmic redistricting across three dimensions: representation equality, compactness, and partisan outcomes. Our findings show that county-based systems eliminate gerrymandering entirely while maintaining comparable representation quality, but require House procedural innovations to implement weighted voting. This analysis provides the first rigorous assessment of county-based federal representation and reveals fundamental trade-offs between boundary permanence and procedural complexity.
\end{abstract}
