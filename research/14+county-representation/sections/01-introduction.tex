Congressional redistricting has become one of the most contentious processes in American democracy. Every decade, following the census, state legislatures and commissions must redraw district boundaries to reflect population changes. This process generates extensive litigation, partisan conflict, and public skepticism about the fairness of electoral outcomes. The fundamental problem is not the complexity of the redistricting algorithms or the intentions of the map-drawers, but the very act of drawing artificial boundaries that can be manipulated for political advantage.
