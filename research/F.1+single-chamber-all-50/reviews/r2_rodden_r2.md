## Response to Revision

The revision addresses my R1 concerns partially. The Wesberry citation correction is clean and appropriate. The new reproducibility note in Section 3 adds important transparency about data prerequisites. However, my primary concern — the absence of partisan outcome analysis from the main empirical paper — remains unaddressed.

**C1 (Partisan outcome analysis)** — Not addressed. The paper still explicitly disclaims partisan outcome analysis ("outside the scope of this paper"), deferring entirely to F.2. For a 50-state empirical paper motivated by state legislative gerrymandering, the absence of any partisan seat-share figures from the primary paper is a continuing gap. I note that F.2 provides partisan results for 30 states using the same Kuriwaki precinct data — the data required for F.1's partisan analysis already exists. A single summary table showing algorithmic versus enacted seat shares for the 30 states where this data is available would complete the primary paper.

**C2 (Enacted map comparison description)** — Not addressed. The 12 states without enacted map comparison data are still not identified by name. The issue I raised in R1 — whether the missing states are non-randomly distributed with respect to gerrymandering — remains unanswered.

**C4 (Cross-reference to F.2 Wisconsin)** — Addressed. The revised Section 6 now includes a cross-reference to F.2's Wisconsin partisan outcome (43D/56R under NestSection, within the gerrymandering context). This was a minor concern and is now handled.

## Assessment

The Wesberry correction and the methodology transparency improvements represent real progress. The remaining partisan outcome gap is the main limitation of this paper for policy and litigation purposes. I maintain 3/4: this is a good empirical paper that would be excellent with the addition of the partisan seat-share analysis that the available data would support.

**Score**: 3/4
**Recommendation**: Accept with minor revisions
