\begin{abstract}
\noindent
Standard ensemble redistricting methods---\recom{}, Forest \recom{},
Merge-Split---sample the full space of valid plans, including plans that
dilute minority voting strength in violation of Section~2 of the Voting
Rights Act~\citep{gingles1986}.
VRA compliance is currently enforced at the \emph{structure layer}
(\vraSection{}, D.0~\citep{dellaLibera2026vraSection}), which produces a single
compliant plan but cannot sample the distribution over compliant alternatives.
We introduce \vr{}: a search-layer algorithm that samples the \emph{conditional
distribution} over plans preserving all designated majority-minority districts.
\vr{} extends \fr{}~\citep{dellaLibera2026forestRecom} with a hard rejection
rule: if a proposed step would reduce the minority VAP fraction of any
protected district below \texttt{vap\_threshold} (default $0.50$), the step
is rejected unconditionally before the Metropolis-Hastings evaluation.
Protected districts are identified once at chain construction from the initial
plan, matching the legal standard that the submitted plan's majority-minority
districts must be preserved.
VRA rejection introduces a three-way step accounting identity:
$\text{accepted} + \text{vra\_rejections} + \text{mh\_rejections} = \text{steps\_taken}$,
enabling full audit of chain behaviour.
Seeding is identical to \fr{} (prefix constants \texttt{FR\_FORWARD\_} and
\texttt{FR\_REVERSE\_}), so the proposal sequence is auditable against an
unconstrained replay.
On North Carolina ($k = 14$, 2 protected districts), Georgia ($k = 14$,
3 protected districts), and Texas ($k = 38$, 7 protected districts),
\vr{} achieves VRA rejection rates of $15$--$25\%$, with EC distributions
slightly above the unconstrained chain, confirming that VRA-compliant plans
occupy a proper sub-region of the full plan space.
When no protected districts exist, \vr{} reduces to \fr{} within $2\%$
on all metrics.
The recommended workflow pairs \vraSection{} (structure layer, for a
guaranteed compliant starting plan) with \vr{} (search layer, for sampling
compliant alternatives): \texttt{--structure ratio-optimal-vra --weights-override
vra-aligned --search vra-recom}.
\end{abstract}
