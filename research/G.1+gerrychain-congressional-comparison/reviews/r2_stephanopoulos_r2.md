# Review: G.1 — GerryChain Congressional Comparison
**Reviewer**: Nicholas Stephanopoulos (Election law, redistricting doctrine)
**Round**: 2
**Score**: 3/4

## Summary

The correction of the NC PP score from 0.412 to 0.337 resolves the arithmetic problem that was most damaging to this paper's credibility for litigation use. The attribution fixes are appropriate. My main legal concerns from Round 1 — the VRA section, Allen v. Milligan, and the 70th percentile threshold disclaimer — have not been addressed in this revision. The paper is significantly improved but still requires those additions before it is ready for litigation deployment.

## Changes Evaluated

**NC PP correction:** Resolved and adequate. The corrected calculation in Section 4.2 using 0.337 is arithmetically consistent with the stated ensemble parameters and the 68th percentile claim. The explanation of the normal-approximation vs. empirical-count distinction is acceptable for a legal audience.

**TX/CA attribution:** Resolved. The Autry 2021 citation for TX and the author-estimate label for CA are both appropriate for the litigation context — they are honest about the evidence quality.

**"Exactly one AR plan" precision:** This has been addressed in G.0 and carried over. The SHA-256 seed sequence specification satisfies the precision concern.

## Outstanding Legal Concerns

**VRA section (H3 from REVISION-PLAN): Not added.** For litigation use, the omission of VRA analysis for the MM district results is a significant gap. The NC 71st percentile MM result means the AR plan concentrates minority voters more than 71% of random valid plans. Under Section 2, a VRA plaintiff could use this to argue packing. The paper makes this result visible in the summary table but does not analyze its VRA implications. In any litigation where G.1 is entered as expert evidence, opposing counsel will immediately focus on this number. The paper needs Section 8.1 on Minority Representation and VRA Compliance.

**Allen v. Milligan (H4): Not added.** Allen v. Milligan (2023) confirmed that Section 2 VRA claims remain viable and that states must create opportunity districts where the Gingles preconditions are met. A paper on redistricting litigation that does not engage with the most important recent Supreme Court VRA decision is legally dated.

**70th percentile threshold disclaimer (M1 from REVISION-PLAN): Not added.** The disclaimer that the [30,70] percentile range is a practical heuristic, not a constitutional benchmark, still has not been added. This remains a risk: lawyers will cite the table without the disclaimer.

## Recommendation

Accept with major revisions on the legal content. The statistical core is now sound. The VRA section and Allen v. Milligan engagement are required before this paper can be cited in litigation without creating additional liability for the author.
