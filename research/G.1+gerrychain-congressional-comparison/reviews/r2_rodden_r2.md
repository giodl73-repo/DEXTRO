## Unresolved Concerns

**NC baseline mismatch (H1 from REVISION-PLAN): Not addressed.** This is the most important unresolved issue. Herschlag 2020 uses 2016 precinct geography and 2016 presidential vote. The AR plan uses 2020 census tracts and 2020 vote. The paper still reads: "the geographic distribution of partisan outcomes is similar, and the percentile estimates are robust to this choice" without demonstrating this. The 2020 NC presidential vote was approximately 48.6% Democratic vs. 47.0% in 2016 — a 1.6pp shift. For a 14-seat delegation, this could shift the ensemble mean partisan seat share by approximately 0.5 seats. The paper's claim of robustness is plausible but unverified. A minimum fix is to add the sensitivity check that the REVISION-PLAN specified: "Repeating the NC analysis with the AR plan evaluated under the 2016 presidential baseline..." If the author does not have access to the Herschlag ensemble directly, the appropriate response is to acknowledge the baseline difference and flag the partisan percentile as approximate.

**WI 61st percentile explanation (H2 from REVISION-PLAN): Not added.** This one-sentence clarification remains missing.

**VRA/MM discussion (H3 from REVISION-PLAN): Not added.** The requested Section 8.1 on minority representation percentiles and VRA implications has not been added.

**Allen v. Milligan (H4 from REVISION-PLAN): Not added.**

Given that four of the five high-priority items from the REVISION-PLAN remain unaddressed, the paper has made only the single most critical fix (NC PP score). The score of 3/4 reflects that the paper's core empirical claims are now internally consistent, but it is not yet fully revised to the standard the REVISION-PLAN set.
