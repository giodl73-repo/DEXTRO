\begin{abstract}
\noindent
G.14 compared eight search-layer algorithms for redistricting ensembles.
G.15 extends the comparison to the complete portfolio of 15 implemented
algorithms across both structure and search layers of the three-layer compositor.
Structure-layer algorithms (\metis{} baseline, Simulated Annealing, CVD
Graph-Distance, CVD Geographic, BFS Growth, and ILP) produce the initial
bisection plan; search-layer algorithms then select among plans.
We evaluate all 15 algorithms on five states --- North Carolina ($k = 14$),
Wisconsin ($k = 8$), Texas ($k = 38$), Florida ($k = 28$), and New Hampshire
($k = 2$) --- measuring five metrics: edge-cut compactness (\ec{}), mean
Polsby-Popper (\pp{}), Democratic seat count (D-seat), lag-100 autocorrelation
(\acf(100)), and wall-clock runtime.
Key findings: ILP certifies global \ec{}-optimality for NH ($k = 2$);
Simulated Annealing achieves the best heuristic \ec{} among structure methods
at 12$\times$ the METIS runtime; CVD-Geographic provides the best \pp{} among
structure methods when centroids are available; BFS Growth is the fastest
structure method ($O(n \log n)$) with competitive \pp{}.
Among search methods, Parallel Tempering outperforms Short-Burst on EC for
TX ($k = 38$) by escaping local optima inaccessible to burst strategies;
SMC-Percentile achieves the highest \pp{} among search methods by selecting
the population-calibrated median plan; and Adaptive Multi-scale matches
or exceeds fixed Multi-scale on TX while self-tuning the coarse fraction.
The primary contribution is a six-question decision framework that maps
redistricting requirements to the appropriate structure + search combination,
complementing the four-question search-only tree from G.14.
All results are single-run (seed 42) with dagger notation (\dag{}) for new
states and algorithms; established results from G.14 (NC/WI/TX search) are
carried forward.
All FL and NH values are single-run estimates~\textsuperscript{$\dagger$};
NC, WI, and TX search baselines are from G.14 multi-seed experiments.
\end{abstract}
