## Response to Round 1 Concerns

### Partisan neutrality claim — Partially Addressed

The revised Section 4.4 now explicitly cites the Rodden geographic-sorting effect and acknowledges that three states are insufficient to establish partisan neutrality as a general property. The sentence "three states are insufficient to establish partisan neutrality as a general property" is the correct hedge I requested. The framing of the WI shift as "consistent with the Rodden geographic-sorting effect" is also correct and appropriately grounded.

However, the text introduces a minor inconsistency: the partisan outcomes paragraph (lines 144-157) mentions NC, GA, and PA as states where outcomes are stable ("Partisan outcomes are stable across percentile levels $p$ for NC, GA, PA"), but the paper only presents data for NC, WI, and TX. GA and PA appear here without any supporting table. This is a factual error introduced by the revision: the authors appear to have drafted the paragraph for a broader evaluation (S-4) that was not implemented. Either (a) remove the GA/PA references and restrict the stability claim to NC alone, or (b) add GA and PA to Table 3.

### Percentile sensitivity argument — Addressed (Satisfactory)

The prospective sensitivity-analysis protocol in Section 5.3 is exactly what I requested and is more legally robust than the retrospective NC stability argument. The explicit statement "a result that varies with $p$ must be disclosed as such" is the right commitment.

### Geographic sorting engagement — Adequately Addressed

The revised paper now engages with the Rodden (2019) reference for the WI partisan shift. The characterization is appropriately cautious: "consistent with the Rodden geographic-sorting effect" rather than a strong causal claim.

### MultiSeedMETIS baseline — Not Addressed

I noted in Round 1 (S-2) that the natural comparison is MultiSeedMETIS($N=100$) to isolate the contribution of the ReCom chain from seed-level METIS diversity. This was listed as a P2 item in the revision plan and remains absent. I acknowledge this is high-effort, but the paper's compactness and partisan claims are weakened without it. For a minor revision, I would accept a discussion paragraph in Section 4.2 noting that this comparison is deferred to future work, with an explicit statement of what the absence of this comparison implies about the claims.

## Minor Points

- Section 4.4 line 144: "NC, GA, PA" — GA and PA data are not in the paper. This must be corrected before publication.
- The paper still does not engage with Chen and Rodden (2013) on geographic vote dilution, which is directly relevant to the compactness-partisan link. A brief acknowledgment in Section 4.4 or the Related Work section would strengthen the paper.

## Overall Assessment

The revision substantially addresses the partisan framing concerns. The GA/PA factual error needs correction. The MultiSeedMETIS baseline remains absent but is a future-work deferral I can accept with an explicit acknowledgment. The paper is close to ready for acceptance.

**Score**: 3.0/4
