## 💡 Lessons from Review Process

### What Worked Well

**1. Interdisciplinary Panel**
- 5 reviewers from 3 disciplines (political science, law, mathematics)
- Cross-cutting perspectives identified blind spots
- Legal reviewers caught constitutional issues CS reviewers missed

**2. Structured P1/P2/P3 Prioritization**
- Clear distinction between blocking (P1) and important (P2)
- Enabled focused revision strategy
- Authors knew exactly what was required vs. optional

**3. Round-by-Round Improvement**
- Round 1: Identify all issues (2.7/4)
- Round 2: Validate P1 fixes, identify remaining gaps (3.4/4)
- Final: Address PP items for premier venue (3.8/4)

**4. Specific, Actionable Feedback**
- "Add Table 3 comparing enacted plans" (not "discuss prior work")
- "Compute Moran's I for each state" (not "quantify clustering")
- Clear success criteria enabled efficient revision

### What Was Challenging

**1. Partisan Analysis Repeatedly Deferred**
- All 5 reviewers flagged in Round 1
- Still absent in Round 2
- Finally addressed in PP1 phase
- **Lesson**: Address unanimous concerns immediately

**2. Scope Creep Risk**
- Reviewers wanted ensemble analysis, resolution testing, additional states
- Authors had to prioritize P1/PP1 over P3 items
- **Lesson**: Focus on blocking items, defer nice-to-haves

**3. Interdisciplinary Translation**
- Mathematical reviewers wanted edge-cut → PP flip
- Legal reviewers wanted constitutional doctrine
- Political scientists wanted partisan analysis
- **Lesson**: Premier venues require satisfying all disciplines
