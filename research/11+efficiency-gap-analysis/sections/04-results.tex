\section{Results}

\subsection{National Summary}

Across all 50 states and three election years (2016, 2018, 2020):
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Algorithmic plans}: Mean efficiency gap = $-3.2\%$ (median: $-3.1\%$)
\item \textbf{Enacted plans}: Mean efficiency gap = $+5.1\%$ (median: $+5.3\%$)
\item \textbf{Difference}: 8.3 percentage points (62\% bias reduction)
\end{itemize}

This represents substantial improvement in partisan fairness while maintaining neutral redistricting criteria.

\subsection{Regional Patterns}

\subsubsection{Rust Belt States}
Pennsylvania, Wisconsin, Michigan show largest disparities:
\begin{itemize}
\item Enacted: Mean EG = $+7.2\%$ (strong R advantage)
\item Algorithmic: Mean EG = $-2.8\%$ (modest D advantage)
\item Difference: 10.0 percentage points
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Sunbelt States}
Arizona, Georgia, North Carolina show consistent but smaller patterns:
\begin{itemize}
\item Enacted: Mean EG = $+4.3\%$ (R advantage)
\item Algorithmic: Mean EG = $-3.1\%$ (D advantage)
\item Difference: 7.4 percentage points
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Temporal Stability}

Efficiency gaps remain consistent across election years, with coefficient of variation < 0.15 for most states. This indicates partisan bias patterns reflect durable geography rather than election-specific volatility.
