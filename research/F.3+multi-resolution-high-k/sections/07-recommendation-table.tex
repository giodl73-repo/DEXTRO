\paragraph{Summary.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item 8 states (16\%) can use tract resolution: California, Florida, Illinois,
    Michigan, New Jersey, New York, Ohio, Texas.
  \item 39 states (78\%) require block-group resolution.
  \item 3 states (6\%) require block resolution (NH, VT, WY).
\end{itemize}

This distribution is notably skewed: most states require at least block-group
resolution for their lower chambers. This reflects the historical design of
census tracts, which were calibrated for analysis at the county/community
level, not for redistricting at sub-congressional scales. The F track's
primary data-infrastructure contribution is the block-group adjacency dataset
that enables 39-state redistricting at appropriate resolution.
