## The 42% VRA Threshold: Legal Framing

Section 3 describes the 42% minority-population threshold as determining where "proportional majority-minority representation through algorithmic redistricting" is achievable. The underlying Paper D.1 includes an important qualification that this guide elides: the threshold is an *empirical regularity*, not a legal bright line, and the VRA Section 2 analysis still requires totality-of-circumstances review under *Thornburg v. Gingles* (1986). Paper D.1 explicitly states: "Our threshold informs legal analysis by demonstrating geographic feasibility patterns, but courts determine actual compliance requirements based on totality-of-circumstances analysis."

For a guide aimed at judges and court personnel, presenting 42% as a definitive threshold without this qualification is legally incomplete and risks being used improperly in litigation. I strongly recommend adding a sentence: "This threshold is an empirical regularity, not a legal bright line; Section 2 analysis still requires the full Gingles three-prong test."
