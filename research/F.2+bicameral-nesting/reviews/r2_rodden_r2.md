## Response to Revision

The state count correction is the most important fix in this revision. The paper now consistently states 41 compatible / 8 incompatible. The California constitutional claim has been corrected: the introduction now accurately describes the historical pre-2008 nesting requirement and the current independent CRC drawing process. These were my C3 and Stephanopoulos's C1 concerns respectively.

**C1 (Wisconsin political context)** — Addressed. Section 6 now includes the footnote noting that Wisconsin's minority-Democratic outcome reflects geographic sorting of the Wisconsin electorate, and that the 1-seat NestSection penalty does not change the fundamental partisan composition of the chamber. This footnote correctly situates the Wisconsin result in the gerrymandering context.

**C3 (Constitutional barriers)** — Addressed. The conclusion and Section 6.3 now note that changing chamber sizes to achieve NestSection compatibility typically requires a constitutional amendment for most states. The phrase "small reforms" has been replaced with language acknowledging the constitutional barrier. This is the correction I requested.

**C2 (12 states without precinct data)** — Not addressed. The partisan analysis still covers 30 of 41 compatible states without identifying the 11 excluded states. A table or footnote identifying the excluded states would enable readers to assess whether the 30-state sample is representative.

**C4 (Competitive super-regions)** — Not addressed. The paper still does not report the number of competitive spine super-regions to bound the scope of the partisan neutrality claim. This remains a methodological gap but is not a blocking issue.

## Assessment

The count correction and legal corrections are the right changes. The paper is now accurate and the conclusions are defensible. I maintain 3/4 — it would reach 4/4 with the addition of the identified-states footnote for the partisan analysis sample.

**Score**: 3/4
**Recommendation**: Accept with minor revisions
