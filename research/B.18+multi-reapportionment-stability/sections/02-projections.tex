\subsection{The Role of Prime Factorization}

The \ar{} bisection tree for $k$ districts is constructed as follows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Find the prime factorisation $k = p_1^{a_1} \times \cdots \times p_r^{a_r}$.
\item At each node of the tree, split by the largest prime factor.
\item Recurse on each half until all leaves have one district.
\end{enumerate}

A change from composite $k$ to prime $k'$ fundamentally changes this tree:
a composite $k$ has a multi-level tree, while a prime $k'$ has a flat tree
(one root split into $k'$ leaves directly).
This is the Texas and Florida case.

\subsection{Projection Uncertainty}

The 2030 seat counts are projections, not final values.
Census Bureau population projections have uncertainty bounds of
$\pm 1$--$2$ seats for the largest states.
Our analysis treats the projected seat counts as exact; the structural
conclusions are robust to small changes in the projections.
