## Legal Framework

### Section 2 of Voting Rights Act (52 U.S.C. § 10301)

**Key Provisions**:
- Prohibits voting practices that **result in** denial or abridgement of right to vote
- **Effects-based** standard (intent irrelevant)
- Applies to **all states** (not just formerly covered jurisdictions)

### Thornburg v. Gingles (1986) - Three Preconditions

To establish Section 2 violation, plaintiffs must show:

1. **Numerosity & Compactness**: Minority group is sufficiently large and geographically compact to constitute majority in single-member district

2. **Political Cohesion**: Minority group is politically cohesive (votes as bloc)

3. **Racially Polarized Voting**: White majority votes sufficiently as bloc to usually defeat minority-preferred candidates

**Implication**: If these conditions exist, failure to create additional opportunity districts may violate Section 2.

### Recent Case Law

**Allen v. Milligan (2023)**:
- Alabama's 1 majority-Black district (out of 7) violated Section 2
- Court required 2nd majority-Black district
- **Rejected compactness defense**: State argued 2nd district would be non-compact
- **Key holding**: Section 2 trumps traditional redistricting criteria

**Cooper v. Harris (2017)**:
- Struck down racial gerrymandering in NC
- BUT: VRA can require consideration of race to prevent vote dilution
- Tension: Must consider race (Section 2) but cannot predominate (Equal Protection)

**Bartlett v. Strickland (2009)**:
- Section 2 does not require "coalition districts" (minority + crossover < 50%)
- Only applies to districts where minority is **majority** of voting-age population (VAP)
