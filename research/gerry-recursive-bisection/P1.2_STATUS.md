### Packing vs Distribution Philosophy

**Enacted Strategy (Traditional VRA Approach)**:
- Pack minority voters into fewer supermajority districts
- Example: Georgia 5 districts at 70-75% Black

**Algorithmic Strategy (Geographic Distribution)**:
- Distribute minority voters across more moderate-majority districts
- Example: Georgia 9 districts at 53-57% non-white

**Political Science Question**: Which is better for minority representation?
- Fewer supermajority districts (70%+) that guarantee wins?
- More moderate-majority districts (53-57%) that increase influence?

### Alabama Edge Case

**Why Algorithm Falls Short**:
- District 6: 48.3% non-white (1.7% short of 50% threshold)
- Geographic constraint: Black population too dispersed to reach 50%+ while maintaining compactness
- Enacted plans pack Black voters into 2 districts at 62% (supermajorities)
