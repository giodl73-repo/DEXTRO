## New Contributions

**The partisan paragraph is the paper's single most important revision.** Without empirical evidence that RB and n-way produce equivalent partisan outcomes, the paper's legal-defensibility argument was circular. With Section 5.4, the argument closes correctly: (1) RB wins on compactness, (2) both strategies produce equivalent partisan outcomes, (3) therefore the compactness-justified choice of RB does not constitute a partisan choice. This three-step argument is now complete.

**The k-bin table (Table 2) is a satisfactory replacement for the missing Figure 1.** The four k-bins provide sufficient resolution to establish flatness across the k range, and the range columns show that the advantage is consistent. Liang's R1 concern about the missing figure is resolved.
