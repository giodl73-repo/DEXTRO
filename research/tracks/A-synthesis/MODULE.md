## Tracks

### Track capstone

**Theme**: Can the 60+ papers of this program be synthesized into a coherent case that speaks to scientists, practitioners, legal experts, and the public simultaneously?

**Chain**: `A.0+synthesis-metapaper` → `A.1+portfolio-guide` → `A.2+portfolio-summary` → `A.3+portfolio-visualization` → `A.4+replication-materials` → `A.5+policy-brief`

**Arc**: The synthesis track begins with the national-scale demonstration (A.0) — the integrating metapaper that draws on all 60+ empirical results to establish algorithmic redistricting as scientifically sound and legally defensible at scale. A.1 provides a researcher's navigation guide to the portfolio, A.2 condenses the headline findings for rapid briefing, A.3 translates results into visual form for non-technical audiences, A.4 assembles AEA-compliant replication materials, and A.5 distills the program into a 4-page policy brief targeting legislative staff and redistricting commissioners.

## Module Arc

Track A is the capstone of the program, not a research track in its own right. Its papers do not generate new empirical findings — they synthesize findings from Tracks B through H and translate them across audiences. The quality of Track A depends entirely on the quality of the underlying tracks: A.0 is only as strong as the 60+ papers it integrates, and A.5 is only as credible as the legal and empirical work in Tracks C, D, and G. The primary cross-track dependency is A.0 → every other track; the primary weakness to assess is whether the synthesis faithfully represents (and does not overstate) the underlying findings.
