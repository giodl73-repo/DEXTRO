## Evaluation of Revisions

**P1.1 (VRA as fifth property)**: Fully and appropriately resolved. Property 5's restriction to the 5 Section 5-historically-covered states (AL, GA, LA, MS, SC) is legally precise: these are the states where VRA compliance is most contested and where a validation failure would be most damaging. The 4/5 success rate is correctly presented as a finding rather than a certification. The Louisiana failure mode (dispersed minority geography) is identified and implicitly connected to C.1's resolution findings, which is the right cross-reference. The citation to D.1's 42% threshold finding is appropriate for grounding the claim in the portfolio's existing empirical work.

**P1.3 (DIA legal status)**: Fully resolved. The footnote at first DIA mention in Section 1.3 is appropriately placed, concise, and accurate. The phrase "has not been enacted by any legislature and is not a binding legal requirement" is legally correct. The bracketed note that "References throughout this paper to DIA 'requirements' describe the standards the model statute would impose if adopted" correctly frames the paper's use of DIA as a normative benchmark rather than a positive legal obligation.

**P1.2 (7% EG threshold context)**: Not explicitly revised. This was a request to add context about the Whitford v. Gill provenance of the 7% threshold. The text in Section 5 does not appear to have been revised. However, this item was not in the top-tier revision priorities and its absence does not change my assessment significantly.

**DIA compliance language in Section 6.3**: Partially addressed. The DIA footnote at introduction corrects the most important instance of the legal-status problem. The body text in Section 6.3 still uses "Satisfied by" language, which Rodden correctly notes is slightly overstated. However, the footnote's framing of DIA requirements as normative rather than positive is sufficient context for a sophisticated reader to interpret "Satisfied by" as "provides evidence toward satisfying." I would mildly prefer the language be updated, but I do not consider it a blocking issue.
