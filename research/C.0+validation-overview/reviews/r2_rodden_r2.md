## Summary

The authors have made targeted revisions addressing two of the four issues I raised. The DIA footnote is appropriate and the bootstrap CI for the variance ratio is a genuine improvement. The addition of Property 5 for VRA is a meaningful expansion of the validation framework. However, my primary P1 concern about the "demographic neutrality" claim (Property 3) remains unaddressed: the paper still asserts that geographic variance dominating temporal variance establishes demographic neutrality, without testing whether states with high demographic change show different temporal variance than states with stable demographics. This is not a minor editorial issue — it is the core empirical gap in Property 3's validation claim. I also note that the DIA qualification language in Section 6.3 ("satisfies" vs. "provides evidence toward satisfying") does not appear to have been revised; the body text still uses "satisfies" in the DIA compliance mapping.

## Evaluation of Revisions

**P1.3 (DIA compliance overstatement)**: Partially addressed. The abstract and introduction now carry the DIA legal-status footnote, which is an improvement. However, Section 6.3's body text still states that R2 is "Satisfied by P1" and R3 is "Satisfied by P2, P3, P4." The revision plan called for replacing "satisfies" with "provides empirical evidence toward satisfying" throughout Section 6.3. This has not been done. For a paper that will be cited in legal proceedings, the distinction between "satisfies a requirement" and "provides evidence toward satisfying a requirement" is legally material.

**P1.1 (Demographic neutrality empirical gap)**: Not addressed. The new definitional paragraph in Section 4 distinguishes metric from boundary stability, which is a different issue from the one I raised. My concern was with Property 3 specifically: the paper claims demographic neutrality based on the geographic-temporal variance decomposition, but this decomposition does not rule out that geographic PP clusters are correlated with racial/ethnic composition. The text of Property 3 and Section 6.2 is unchanged from Round 1.

**P1.2 (Competitive-state selection bias)**: Not addressed in body text. The 15-state restriction acknowledgment in Section 6.4 was already present in Round 1. The request was for a sentence at the start of Section 5 noting the restriction and its implications for national representativeness. This has not been added.

**Bootstrap CI (Liang's P1.1)**: Resolved. The [2.1×, 4.8×] CI for the variance ratio is a genuine improvement.

**DIA footnote (Stephanopoulos's P1.3)**: Resolved.

**Property 5 / VRA (Stephanopoulos's P1.1)**: Adequate for Round 2. The new Property 5 is appropriately scoped and hedged.

## Remaining Concerns

The unresolved items are P1.3 from Stephanopoulos (DIA compliance language in Section 6.3 body text) and my P1.1 (demographic neutrality empirical gap). Of these, the DIA language fix is straightforward. The demographic neutrality gap would require either a direct test or a reframing of Property 3 as "geographic dominance" rather than "demographic neutrality." Without addressing at least the DIA language issue, I cannot raise my score to 4.
