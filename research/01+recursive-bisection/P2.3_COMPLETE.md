### Finding 1: **Process Type Determines Performance**

| Process Type | States | Algo PP | Enacted PP | Gap |

**Implication**: Largest gap vs. commission maps (which explicitly optimize compactness), smallest gap vs. court maps (neutral but not compactness-focused).

### Finding 2: **Michigan Commission Trade-offs**

| Criterion | Algorithmic | Enacted (Commission) |

**Implication**: Commission achieved exceptional compactness through multi-criteria optimization, but at cost of increased subdivision splits. Demonstrates human expertise but also reveals trade-offs.

### Finding 3: **Illinois Gerrymander - Algorithm's Strongest Case**

- **Enacted PP**: 0.148 (lowest nationally, Democratic gerrymander)
- **Algorithmic PP**: 0.240
- **Improvement**: **+62.2%**

**Implication**: Where it matters most (preventing partisan manipulation), algorithm provides dramatic improvements. This is the core value proposition.

### Finding 4: **Texas Anomaly Explained**

- **Enacted PP**: 0.189 (Republican-controlled)
- **Algorithmic PP**: 0.163
- **Difference**: -13.8% (algorithm worse)

**Why?**
1. Geographic constraints dominate (sprawling state, uneven population)
2. Gerrymandering through demographic engineering (not geometric distortion)
3. Low compactness is fundamentally geographic, not primarily partisan

**Implication**: Algorithm prevents geometric manipulation but cannot overcome inherent geographic constraints.

### Finding 5: **Minnesota Court Map Equivalence**

- **Court-Drawn PP**: 0.320
- **Algorithmic PP**: 0.325
- **Difference**: +1.6% (statistical tie)

**Implication**: In favorable geographic contexts, neutral processes (court or algorithm) converge on similar outcomes. Validates both approaches.

## Conceptual Frameworks Introduced

### 1. **Redistricting Reform Trilemma**

Three competing objectives (cannot maximize all simultaneously):
1. **Maximum Compactness**: Commission-drawn maps (requires constitutional amendments, months of work)
2. **Maximum Transparency**: Algorithmic approaches (deterministic, reproducible, immune to manipulation)
3. **Maximum Accessibility**: Immediate adoption without political barriers

**Implication**: Forces explicit choice about which dimensions to prioritize. Algorithm chooses transparency + accessibility over maximum compactness.

### 2. **Context-Dependent Value Proposition**

Algorithm performance depends on comparison benchmark:
- **vs. Gerrymanders**: Dramatic improvement (where reform needed most)
- **vs. Court maps**: Competitive performance
- **vs. Commissions**: Lower compactness (but commissions rare, politically difficult)

**Implication**: Algorithmic value is highest precisely where current practice is worst (gerrymandered states).

### 3. **Geometric vs. Demographic Manipulation**

Two gerrymandering mechanisms:
1. **Geometric distortion**: Bizarre shapes to pack/crack voters (Illinois)
2. **Demographic engineering**: Compact districts combining disparate communities (Texas)

**Implication**: Algorithm prevents #1 (cannot see partisan data) but cannot prevent #2 if it arises from neutral compactness optimization.
