# Review: G.0 — Ensemble Methodology
**Reviewer**: Nicholas Stephanopoulos (Election law, redistricting doctrine)
**Round**: 2
**Score**: 3/4

## Summary

The revision addresses all my Round 1 concerns adequately. The legal sections are now more accurate, and the "exactly one AR plan" claim has been given the precision it needed. The paper is ready for publication as a framework document, though one minor legal accuracy issue remains.

## Changes Evaluated

**Post-Rucho legal landscape:** The revised Section 6 now correctly distinguishes federal court limitations from state court redistricting claims. The explicit statement that "Federal courts may not use ensemble evidence for partisan gerrymandering claims after Rucho" followed by the state court examples (PA LWV, NC Harper) is legally accurate. The earlier version implied broader judicial acceptance than the case law supported.

**"Exactly one AR plan" precision:** The revised language — "There is exactly one AR plan produced by the CS algorithm at T=600 from the SHA-256-derived seed sequence and the specified census release. This plan is a local optimum under METIS, reached deterministically from the canonical seed" — is precise and legally defensible. This addresses my Round 1 concern that a challenge could attack the uniqueness claim. The acknowledgment that it is a local (not global) optimum is honest and will hold up under cross-examination.

**L1 (\est marker definition):** The definition box at the end of Section 3 has been added. Good.

## Remaining Issue

Section 6.3 still does not engage with Shaw v. Reno and Vera v. Richards on compactness as a constitutional minimum. I noted this as a moderate-priority issue in Round 1. The paper invokes compactness as corroborative evidence but does not distinguish the Shaw "grotesque shape" threshold from the optimality claim. Adding a sentence acknowledging that the AR plan's 61st–72nd percentile PP position places it well above the constitutional minimum would pre-empt this challenge. This is a refinement rather than a correction, and I do not consider it blocking.

The paper's three-part argument structure (constitutional grounding → not a partisan outlier → reproducible) remains intact and well-ordered. For litigation purposes, this paper is now a credible framework document.

## Recommendation

Accept with minor revisions. The legal accuracy concerns from Round 1 are addressed. The Shaw v. Reno engagement would strengthen the paper but is not required for publication.
