# Review: G.0 — Ensemble Methodology
**Reviewer**: Jonathan Rodden (Political geography, partisan sorting)
**Round**: 2
**Score**: 3/4

## Summary

The Round 2 revision resolves the primary concerns from my Round 1 review. The addition of a paragraph in Section 6.1 explicitly noting that ensemble-median outcomes are not normatively neutral in sorted states is exactly what I asked for, and the framing is accurate. The CS bridge has been corrected. The legal section has been updated to reflect the post-Rucho landscape accurately.

## Specific Changes

**Rodden effect in framework (Section 6.1):** The added paragraph correctly states: "the ensemble median represents what geography determines, not what fairness requires. In geographically sorted states, the ensemble median itself reflects Republican over-representation relative to vote share." This is the essential political science caveat that was missing in Round 1. The cross-reference to G.2 Section 4 for the proportionality corridor analysis is appropriate.

**Legal section (Section 6, post-Rucho):** The added sentence distinguishing federal court limitations from state court viability is accurate. The citation to PA LWV, NC Harper, and NY Harkenrider is correct and current.

**CS bridge:** The revised framing ("orthogonal mechanisms") resolves the false-analogy concern. Political scientists who read the G-track papers will not be misled into thinking T=600 and ESS are commensurable quantities.

**Table 1 compactness weight:** Still shows "None" for all ensemble methods without the Metropolized Forest ReCom footnote that was requested in M1. This is a minor omission that I flag but do not consider blocking.

## Residual Concern

The framework paper still does not explicitly define "geographic baseline" as a calibration parameter (from my Round 1 minor issue). Section 3.4 mentions "electoral baseline" in passing, but the paper should state that holding the electoral baseline constant is required when comparing AR plan percentiles across states or across census years. This is a clarifying addition, not a correction of an error. It would strengthen the paper for readers who want to apply the framework to new states.

## Recommendation

Accept with minor revisions. The blocking and high-priority concerns are resolved. The paper is now adequate as a framework for the G-series.
